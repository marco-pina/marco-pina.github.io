---
title: "The Credibility Cost of Redemption: Local Currency Debt and the Inflation Risk Premium"
date: 2026-01-01
---

Emerging markets increasingly borrow in local currency from foreign investors. This reduces currency mismatch but exposes lenders to inflation risk. We combine an NLP measure of central-bank communication with foreign-ownership data for 15 emerging economies to study how debt structure shapes term-premium responses to policy signals. Tightening signals have no significant association with the term premium when foreign ownership is low, but are associated with a 3.22 basis point increase in the 10-year term premium at the 75th percentile of foreign ownership. The response reflects adverse news about inflationary fundamentals rather than expected tightening. A model in which the government’s incentive to inflate rises with the local-currency debt share rationalizes this pattern.
